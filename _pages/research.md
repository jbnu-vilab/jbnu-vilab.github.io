Our research focuses on image processing and computer vision. Below are the main research areas we are actively working on.

---

### [3D Gaussian Splatting](#3d-gaussian-splatting)

3D Gaussian Splatting is a state-of-the-art technique for novel view synthesis and 3D scene representation. We explore style transfer, geometry-aware rendering, and real-time rendering applications using Gaussian-based scene representations.

---

### [Federated Learning](#federated-learning)

Federated Learning enables collaborative model training across distributed devices without sharing raw data. Our research addresses challenges such as communication efficiency, data heterogeneity, and model aggregation strategies for robust and privacy-preserving learning.

---

### [Depth Estimation](#depth-estimation)

We study monocular and multi-view depth estimation methods, including self-supervised learning frameworks that handle challenging conditions such as image blur and low light.

---

### [Diffusion Models](#diffusion-models)

Diffusion-based generative models are applied to image synthesis, relighting, and data augmentation. We investigate how diffusion priors can be leveraged to improve downstream vision tasks.

---

### [Image Enhancement](#image-enhancement)

Image enhancement encompasses a broad set of tasks including low-light enhancement, contrast adjustment, demoiréing, and super-resolution. We develop deep learning-based approaches that adaptively improve visual quality under diverse degradation conditions.

---

### [Image Quality Assessment](#image-quality-assessment)

Blind image quality assessment (BIQA) aims to predict perceptual quality without a reference image. We design transformer-based and dual-branch architectures that capture both local and global distortion features for accurate quality prediction.
